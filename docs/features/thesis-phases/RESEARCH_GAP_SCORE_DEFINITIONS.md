### Ideal Balance

**Best Thesis Gaps** have:
- âœ… Moderate to high Novelty (60+)
- âœ… High Feasibility (70+)
- âœ… High Significance (75+)

### Trade-offs

| Scenario | Novelty | Feasibility | Significance | Best For |
|----------|---------|-------------|--------------|----------|
| High in all | 80+ | 80+ | 80+ | Ideal thesis |
| High novelty, low feasibility | 90+ | 40 | 75+ | Future research (not now) |
| High feasibility, low significance | 50 | 95+ | 40 | Practice/skill-building |
| High significance, low feasibility | 70 | 30 | 95+ | Advocacy/policy paper |
| All moderate | 60 | 60 | 60 | Safe, achievable thesis |

### Choosing Your Gap

**Choose based on your situation**:

1. **First Thesis/Limited Resources**
   - Prioritize: Feasibility > Significance > Novelty
   - Want: Achievable, meaningful, reasonably new

2. **Strong Academic Background**
   - Prioritize: Novelty > Significance > Feasibility
   - Want: Original, important, challenging

3. **Community-Based Research**
   - Prioritize: Significance > Feasibility > Novelty
   - Want: Matters to community, doable with partners, contributes new insight

4. **Publishing Goal**
   - Prioritize: Novelty > Significance > Feasibility
   - Want: Original findings, important implications, publishable

## FAQ

**Q: What's a "good" novelty score?**
A: 70+ is good, 80+ is excellent. Depends on field - some areas have more prior work.

**Q: Can I pursue a gap with low feasibility?**
A: Not for your thesis! Save low-feasibility high-significance gaps for future research.

**Q: What if all scores are moderate (50-60)?**
A: That's still valid! Moderate gaps are often the safest thesis choice.

**Q: Which score matters most?**
A: Feasibility is critical (must be achievable). Then balance novelty + significance.

**Q: Can scores change during research?**
A: Yes - as you learn more, scores may shift. Use them as starting guidance, not final judgment.
