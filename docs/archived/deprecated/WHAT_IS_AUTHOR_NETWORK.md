## How It Works (Simple Version)

### Input
Papers with author information (from your search)

### Process
1. Extract all authors
2. Find who co-authored papers together
3. Arrange them in visual space:
   - **Larger circles** = published more papers
   - **Greener circles** = collaborated with many others
   - **Lines between** = co-authored papers

### Output
Interactive visualization you can explore

### Result
You understand your research field better

## Common Questions Answered

**Q: How many papers do I need?**
A: At least 10-15 to see useful patterns. More is better.

**Q: Is bigger always better?**
A: Size = volume. Bigger authors published more, but check quality by reading abstracts.

**Q: What if the network is sparse?**
A: Topic might be new or niche. Try broader search terms.

**Q: Should I read every author's papers?**
A: No. Focus on large green nodes first (influential), then expand.

**Q: How long does it take to learn?**
